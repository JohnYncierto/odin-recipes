Fried Chicken

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, breasts)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or peanut oil)

Steps

  1. Pat the chicken pieces dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  2. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs and buttermilk to create an egg wash.
  3. In another shallow bowl, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Dip each chicken piece into the egg wash, making sure it's well coated on all sides.
  5. Transfer the chicken to the flour mixture and coat thoroughly, pressing gently so the seasoning adheres well.
  6. Heat oil in a deep skillet or Dutch oven to 325-350°F (163-177°C). Use a thermometer to ensure proper temperature.
  7. Carefully place the chicken pieces into the hot oil, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding the pan.
  8. Fry for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  9. Remove the chicken with tongs and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  10. Let cool for a few minutes, then serve hot with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits.
